# Quick Fix marketing creative pack

The **AccessRevamp Quick Fix Plan remains $199 USD one time**. The plan now includes an AI-assisted, human-reviewed marketing creative package designed to be inexpensive to produce without giving the customer generic or unreviewed output.

## Included deliverables

The package covers **one business, one campaign, and one promoted offer** unless the written scope says otherwise.

It includes:

1. Two master creative directions for the approved campaign.
2. Five format adaptations for each master direction.
3. Ten final Canva-ready creative variations in total.
4. A recommended headline, supporting line, call to action, caption, channel, and posting context for each master direction.
5. One consolidated revision round after the customer reviews the two master directions.
6. Editable Canva-ready design files plus agreed PNG or PDF exports when the delivery workflow supports them.

## Standard format matrix

Each approved master direction is adapted into:

| Format | Recommended size | Common use |
|---|---:|---|
| Square feed | 1080 × 1080 | Instagram, Facebook, Google Business Profile, general organic social |
| Portrait feed | 1080 × 1350 | Instagram and Facebook feed placements |
| Story / Reel cover | 1080 × 1920 | Instagram Stories, Facebook Stories, Reel or short-video cover |
| Landscape ad | 1200 × 628 | Meta link ads, display placements, website banners |
| Print poster | US Letter or A4 | Counter sign, flyer, handout, community board, local event |

Two master directions multiplied by these five formats produces the ten included variations. A customer may request a different equivalent format mix before production begins, but the total remains ten unless a different scope is approved in writing.

## Low-cost AI-assisted production model

The package is intentionally designed to remain economical:

- Begin from a controlled AccessRevamp layout system rather than designing ten unrelated campaigns from scratch.
- Use AI to help draft headline options, caption options, layout directions, background concepts, and safe image-editing instructions.
- Use the customer’s supplied logo, approved brand colors, product or service images, offer details, and required disclosures.
- Prefer Canva Free-compatible fonts, shapes, icons, and elements so the customer is not forced into an extra paid asset license.
- Build two strong master directions, obtain one consolidated decision, and then adapt each direction into five formats.
- Reuse approved copy and visual components across the size adaptations while preserving legibility, safe margins, and channel-specific hierarchy.
- Keep production asynchronous and template-driven; do not add meetings or revision loops that were not included in the written scope.

AI is a production assistant, not the final approver. A named person must review every delivered creative.

## Required customer inputs

Production should not begin until the following are received or explicitly marked unnecessary:

- Legal or public business name.
- Logo files or permission to reproduce the public logo.
- Approved brand colors and fonts when available.
- Product, service, venue, or team images the customer has the right to use.
- The exact promoted offer, price, dates, restrictions, eligibility, and redemption instructions.
- Required legal, platform, industry, sweepstakes, financing, health, or promotional disclosures.
- Desired call to action and destination URL.
- Primary audience and geographic targeting supplied by the customer or clearly labeled as a recommendation.
- Prohibited claims, imagery, competitor references, and brand constraints.

Never invent a discount, deadline, inventory statement, testimonial, performance claim, guarantee, customer result, or legal disclosure.

## Human quality gates

Before delivery, verify every variation for:

- Correct business, campaign, offer, price, dates, and call to action.
- Accurate spelling, punctuation, phone numbers, URLs, promo codes, and disclosures.
- Brand resemblance without falsely implying endorsement by another company.
- Text legibility at the final output size.
- Sufficient contrast and a logical reading order.
- Safe margins and no cropped logo, text, faces, products, or required disclosures.
- Appropriate alt-text guidance for digital posting.
- No unsupported revenue, ranking, legal-compliance, security, health, or performance claim.
- No copied competitor creative, unlicensed stock, unauthorized celebrity likeness, trademark misuse, or hidden watermark.
- No AI-generated text embedded incorrectly in an image.
- Successful Canva opening and agreed export generation.
- A named reviewer, review timestamp, and final approval status.

## Tracking fields

Each creative should be stored with:

- Creative ID.
- Project ID.
- Customer email.
- Website URL.
- Campaign or offer.
- Creative number from 1 through 10.
- Master direction number 1 or 2.
- Format and dimensions.
- Headline, caption, call to action, and recommended channel.
- Canva URL when available.
- Export or Drive URL.
- AI-assisted flag.
- Rights review status.
- Customer approval status.
- Human reviewer and review timestamp.
- Revision notes.
- Delivery status and delivered timestamp.

Use stable IDs and never overwrite another customer’s assets.

## Delivery timing

The reviewed website analysis, prioritized recommendations, and action plan follow the approved three-business-day promise after payment and receipt of required information. The creative brief and two master-direction proposals may be included in that analysis package when the required brand and offer inputs are ready.

The final ten creative variations follow the written Quick Fix implementation schedule because production depends on customer assets, master-direction approval, copy accuracy, and requested revisions. Do not promise that every website implementation and all ten final creatives will be completed within three business days unless the written scope explicitly supports that schedule.

## Exclusions

The $199 plan does not include:

- Ad spend or platform fees.
- Media buying, campaign launch, bidding, targeting configuration, or ongoing optimization.
- Printing, shipping, installation, or physical production.
- Paid Canva elements, paid stock media, or third-party licenses unless the customer pays or approves them.
- Photography, videography, voice-over, complex illustration, or advanced motion design.
- More than one campaign or promoted offer.
- More than two master directions, ten final variations, or one consolidated revision round.
- Ongoing social posting, community management, or recurring content production.
- Legal review of advertising claims or required disclosures.

Additional work requires a separately written scope and cannot silently create a recurring charge.
