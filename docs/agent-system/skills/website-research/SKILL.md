# Skill: Sourced Website Research

## Goal

Produce a customer-specific evidence base that accurately describes the public website, offer, brand, audience, content, conversion path, and improvement opportunities.

## Inputs

- Approved project ID and plan.
- Confirmed website URL.
- Customer intake, approved email context, and customer-owned files.
- Public reference URLs supplied by the customer.

## Procedure

1. Normalize the canonical public origin and record retrieval time.
2. Inventory public home, product/service, pricing, plan/subscription, about, contact, FAQ, policy, and key conversion pages.
3. Extract exact names, prices, promises, phrases, calls to action, proof, locations, hours, contact methods, and customer journey.
4. Record design evidence: colors, typography, imagery, icons, layout, spacing, and motion.
5. Record conversion evidence: offer clarity, proof, friction, objections, monetization, and next actions.
6. Label every item as quoted fact, observed behavior, inference, recommendation, concept, or verified result.
7. Save each factual item with source URL and retrieval time.
8. Human-review customer-facing claims before use.

## Prohibited actions

Do not log in, submit forms, enter checkout, scrape private routes, evade access controls, guess unpublished contact details, or invent business facts. Do not describe passive signals as confirmed security vulnerabilities or legal violations.

## Output

`WEBSITE_RESEARCH.md` with source table, business profile, offer catalog, exact language bank, brand tokens, page inventory, customer journey, conversion opportunities, risks, unknowns, and questions that require customer confirmation.

## Quality gate

Every important factual claim has a source. Contradictions are called out. Unknowns remain unknown. Customer data never crosses projects. Files remain under 9,000,000 bytes or are split with a manifest.
