# Google Flow — Immersive 3D Scroll-Journey Prompts

These prompts are for three separate **8-second, 16:9, image-to-video clips** made from the supplied website hero screenshots.

## The corrected motion concept

This is **not** a flat webpage scrolling vertically. The screenshot is the opening state of an immersive website. As the viewer “scrolls,” the scroll input drives a **forward camera journey into the hero artwork itself**. The scene gains real depth, the camera travels toward and through the main subject, architectural layers may unfold like a premium miniature diorama or cutaway model, and the viewer is shown around the world inside the website.

Think of the motion language as:

- a high-end scroll-controlled 3D website;
- one continuous camera move, not a montage;
- the hero image becoming a dimensional environment;
- controlled dolly, parallax, cutaway, masking, and spatial reveals;
- interface elements pinned briefly at the beginning, then cleanly masked away;
- no ordinary long-page scroll and no page sections sliding upward.

## Recommended workflow

Use one source screenshot and one matching prompt per generation. Keep the source image as the starting frame. Generate each clip separately at 8 seconds in landscape format.

The most important instruction in every prompt is repeated near the beginning:

> **Do not vertically scroll a flat webpage. Move the camera forward into the pictured world as though the visitor’s mouse wheel is controlling a premium 3D website.**

## Shared quality rules

- Begin with a recognizable match to the source screenshot.
- Preserve the original brand palette and core composition.
- Use one uninterrupted shot with no hard cuts.
- Make the movement smooth, scroll-linked, and deliberately paced rather than like a fast drone flight.
- Show convincing foreground, middle-ground, and background parallax.
- Reveal hidden spaces through doors, columns, hedges, glass, masks, or clean architectural cutaways.
- Keep the existing opening text readable only while it is visible; do not generate new paragraphs or random interface copy.
- Do not animate a human face or stone statue.
- Do not place the experience inside a laptop, browser, phone, or device mockup.
- End inside or overlooking the explored environment, not on another flat webpage section.
